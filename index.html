Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about studying in China

Tuition fees vary by institution and program, but generally range from $2,000 to $10,000 per year for undergraduate programs. Medical programs tend to be at the higher end of this range.

Many universities offer programs taught entirely in English, particularly in STEM and business fields. However, learning basic Chinese is highly recommended for daily life. Some programs may require HSK certification for Chinese-taught programs.

We recommend starting the application process 9-12 months before your intended start date. Most universities have application deadlines between February and June for September intake.

Cost of living varies by city. In smaller cities, you can live comfortably on $100-$250 per month, while in cities like Beijing or Shanghai, you may need $300-$400 per month including accommodation, food, and transportation.
